Week 1 Cybersecurity training

It is essential to have a good understanding of standard Cybersecurity terms. This week there is a set of flashcards that wil provide a great starting point to your cybersecurity journey.

Spear Phising

Click for Definition

Phishing method that targets specific individuals or groups within an organization

Whaling

Click for Definition

Phishing attack that targets high-profile employees, such as the chief executive officer or chief financial officer, in order to steal sensitive information from a company

Malware

Click for Definition

Software specifically designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system.

Encryption

Click for Definition

The process of encoding information to prevent unauthorized access.

Firewall

Click for Definition

A network security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules and policies.

DDoS

Click for Definition

A Distributed Denial of Service attack is a malicious attempt to make a server or a network resource unavailable to users, usually by overwhelming the target or its surrounding infrastructure with a flood of Internet traffic.

Social engineering

Click for Definition

The use of psychological manipulation to trick individuals into divulging confidential information.

Two-factor authentication

Click for Definition

A security process that requires you to provide two different types of identification to access an account or system, typically a password and a code sent to a mobile device.

VPN

Click for Definition

Virtual Private Network - a secure network connection that allows you to access the internet and your organization's network securely, by encrypting your online activities and routing them through a server located elsewhere.
