Week 2 - Password, passwords and more passwords!

Tips on Passwords

  1. Use a unique password for each account: Do not use the same password for different accounts as it makes it easier for hackers to gain access to multiple accounts.
  2. Use long passwords: Passwords should be at least 12 characters long, and the longer the better. The use of a passphrase instead of a password is also recommended.
  3. Include a mix of characters: Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  4. Avoid using personal information: Do not use information that can be easily guessed or obtained, such as your name, birthdate, or phone number.
  5. Use a password manager: Consider using a password manager to create and store unique passwords for each account. This eliminates the need to remember multiple passwords. Bitwarden or KeePass are a great options.
  6. Enable two-factor authentication: Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second form of authentication, such as a code sent to your phone.
