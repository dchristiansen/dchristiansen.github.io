Phishing Awareness

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers use fake emails, text messages, or websites to trick individuals into providing sensitive information, such as login credentials or financial information. The goal of a phishing attack is to steal personal information or money.

How to Spot a Phishing Attempt

  • The email or message requests personal information, such as login credentials or financial information.
  • The sender's email address or phone number is slightly different from the real one.
  • The message contains spelling and grammar errors.
  • The message creates a sense of urgency, such as threatening to close your account if you don't respond quickly.
  • The message contains a suspicious attachment or link.

Tips to Avoid Falling for Phishing Scams

  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails or messages, even if they appear to be from a legitimate source.
  • Don't click on links or download attachments from unknown sources.
  • Be wary of emails or messages that create a sense of urgency.
  • Don't provide personal information or login credentials in response to an email or message.
  • Use anti-phishing software or browser extensions to help identify and block phishing attempts.
